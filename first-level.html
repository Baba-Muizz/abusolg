Sociological Terms & Key Definitions

Achieved Status

A social position (status) obtained through an individual's own talents and efforts.

Anomie

A breakdown or confusion in the norms, values, and culture of a group or a society. A condition of relative normlessness.

Ascribed status

A social position (status) such as sex, race, and social class that a person acquires at birth.

Bureaucracy

A large-scale formal organization with centralized authority, a hierarchical chain of command, explicit rules and procedures, and an emphasis on formal positions rather than on persons.

Capitalism

A form of economic organization in which private individuals accumulate and invest capital, own the means of production, and control profits.
